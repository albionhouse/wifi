The Basic I package is the bare minimum for everyday use and offers typical speeds largely comparable to the real-world performance of ADSL2+. If your internet usage mainly consists of light web browsing, email and the occasional upload of photos to social media sites then you might be perfectly fine with this speed.

Basic II package and vouchers are mid-tier for moderate and budget conscious users, opening the door to streaming video services. However, shared across multiple usages, bandwith can bottleneck.

So what speed do I need?

It very much depends on what you use the internet for.

0.5 - 1 Mbps is typically all you need for receiving and sending emails, as well as general web surfing and social media browsing.

3 - 5 Mbps is roughly what you need for watching videos including streaming services such as Netflix or playing online games of standard quality streams. 

5 - 8 Mbps will get you by if you wish to stream those videos in High Definition. 

8 - 10 Mbps is what you'll need if you’re playing an an online multiplayer game as they will often require more megabits per second for smooth game play, especially if want to ensure that the game doesn't lag and that no other usage is slowed down while you're playing.

25 Mbps is what is recommended if you wish to watch something in Ultra High Definition such as sports in 4K.

What is Mbps? 
Mbps stands for megabits per second and it's how transmissions across computer networks and broadband are measured. A megabit is equal to one million bits.
The higher the Mbps, the quicker your internet will work.
